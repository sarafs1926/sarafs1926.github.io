Final project proposal:

1) Title
~ns TDC with Zynq-7000 SoC

2) Abstract: the concept
Under the supervision of Professor Kearns and Dr. Jin-Yuan Wu of Fermilab I have helped develop prototype front-end electronics for Hyper-Kamiokande, which includes a QTC (charge-to-time converter) and a TDC (time-to-digital converter) on an FPGA. For my 681 final project I will design my own TDC from scratch which will digitize square pulses on the order of 1 ns. I will use the Xilinx Zynq system-on-a-chip, which integrates FPGA architectures with a processor. To program the SoC, I will install PetaLinux and Xilinx Vivado Design Suite on the neutrino buphy machine and work remotely.

3) Parts list.

  • Zynq Zybo eval board (provided by the BU EDF)
  • Laptop (mine)
  • Tektronix Oscilloscope (from my 681 lab bench)
